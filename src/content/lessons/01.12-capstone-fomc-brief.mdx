## Capstone — write the FOMC brief

You've now built the scaffolding to read a Fed decision intelligently. This capstone forces you to use it.

## The assignment

1. Open the most recent FOMC statement: <SourceLink id="fomc-statements" />.
2. Read the statement end to end. (~5 minutes.)
3. If there were dissents, read the dissenting statements.
4. Read the Powell press conference transcript or watch the video. (~30 minutes.)
5. Pull up the dot plot from the most recent SEP (Summary of Economic Projections).
6. Write a 1-page brief covering:
   - **What the Fed did** (rate decision, balance sheet posture).
   - **What the statement signals about their read on inflation, employment, and growth.**
   - **What the dot plot implies about expected near-term path.**
   - **Where you think the consensus is wrong**, or where you'd push back on the Fed's framing, with one specific reason.
   - **What you'd do differently in your portfolio if you took the Fed's projection seriously**, vs if you took a contrarian view.

There is no autograder for this. Compare your brief to the post-FOMC analysis that financial press publishes the next day (WSJ, FT, Bloomberg). You should be able to identify points they cover that you missed, and points you got that they didn't.

The first time, this will be hard. The 10th time, you'll be reading FOMC statements faster than the journalists.

## What this exercise is really doing

Most retail investors form macro views by ingesting opinions about what the Fed did. This exercise inverts that. You form the view first by reading the primary source, then check your read against the commentary. Over time you'll find:

- Your reading converges with the smart commentary.
- You spot points the commentary misses.
- You also notice when the commentary is reaching — picking a narrative because they need to file a piece, not because the statement supported it.

This is the disposition Bernstein gestures at in hurdle #2 ("understand finance basics"). You can't outsource macro views to financial press; you have to develop the reading practice yourself.
