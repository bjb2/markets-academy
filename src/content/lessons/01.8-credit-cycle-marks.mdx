## The cycle that determines severity

The *business* cycle is about GDP and employment. The *credit* cycle is about lender behavior — risk appetite, underwriting standards, the willingness to extend credit. They're correlated but not the same. Severe recessions are credit-cycle events. Mild ones aren't.

Howard Marks's memo "You Can't Predict, You Can Prepare" and the broader frame in his "Best Of" collection (<SourceLink id="marks-memos-best-of" />) is the canonical treatment. Stop here and go read 3–5 of them — at least *You Can't Predict, You Can Prepare* (2001), *The Race to the Bottom* (2007), and *On the Couch* (2016). Each is 10–15 pages.

## The Marks frame

Marks observes that credit cycles oscillate between two extremes:

**Credit cycle TOP:** capital is abundant; lenders compete for borrowers; covenants weaken; spreads tighten; underwriting standards relax; "everyone is a genius." Risk feels low (and so is being priced as low), but objectively the embedded risk in the system is at its highest.

**Credit cycle BOTTOM:** capital is scarce; lenders demand high yields and tight covenants; borrowers can't refinance; defaults rise; "no one wants to touch anything." Risk feels high (and is being priced as high), but objectively the embedded risk on new investments is at its lowest because pricing is so attractive.

The cruel symmetry: when investments feel safest, they're most dangerous, and vice versa. The cycle is driven by the boom-and-bust dynamics of investor psychology layered on top of the real-economy cycle, with credit availability as the lever that amplifies both directions.

## How to read a credit cycle in real time

Watch:

- **HY (high-yield) spreads.** ICE BofA US High Yield Index OAS on FRED. Sub-300 bp = top-of-cycle complacency. 800 bp+ = panic. 500 bp = roughly average.
- **Issuance pace and quality**. Are CCC-rated bonds being issued in volume? PIK toggles? Covenant-lite loan share? These are top-of-cycle markers.
- **Lender talk**. When private credit funds publicly worry they "can't put money to work," that's a top.
- **Default rate** trailing 12 months. Lagging but confirms the cycle's regime.

The credit cycle leads equity drawdowns in severe-recession scenarios. HY spreads widening before the S&P falls has preceded most major sell-offs.

## What Marks does NOT claim

Marks explicitly does not claim to predict the cycle's timing. His operating principle is that we cannot know *when* the turn will come, but we can know *where in the cycle* we are — which tells us how aggressively or defensively to position. Defense in late credit cycles; offense at credit cycle bottoms. Position size as a function of cycle awareness, not forecast confidence.

This is the most important real-world investing posture in the entire curriculum. The longcall + sa-infra sleeves we run are themselves cycle-position-aware: we are buying real economic capex thesis, not a bet on the cycle's exact turn.
