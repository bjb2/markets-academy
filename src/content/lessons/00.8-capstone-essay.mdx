## Capstone — write the essay

This is a synthesis exercise. You will defend or critique the claim:

> *Almost nothing in personal finance matters more than starting to invest early.*

Write ~200 words. There is no autograder. You read your own essay against the rubric, mark yourself correct if it meets the bar, and move on.

## Rubric (what a strong essay covers)

- Acknowledges the compounding math directly: small differences in years compound into large differences in wealth (Lesson 0.4).
- Identifies the principal failure modes that DO matter despite or because of compounding: behavior under drawdown (Bernstein hurdle #4), fee drag (hurdle #5), savings rate ceiling (hurdle #1).
- Resolves the tension: starting early is a *necessary* condition for many outcomes but not a *sufficient* one. Someone who starts at 22 and panic-sells in their first bear market does worse than someone who starts at 32 and holds.
- Offers a falsifiable formulation, not a feel-good one. "Starting early dominates because compounding is exponential and human lifetimes have a fixed end" is concrete. "Starting early is important" is not.

## What to do

1. Open a text editor or paper notebook.
2. Write 200 words. Take 30 minutes. Don't outline; just write.
3. Compare to the rubric.
4. Type a one-sentence summary of your essay below to mark complete.
